Project Overview

This project started from a simple curiosity.
As a student, I often experienced stress related to studies and personal pressures, and I wondered if other students around me were experiencing the same thing. To explore this question, I conducted a small survey in my local community to understand how students were managing sleep, stress, and daily routines.
The responses revealed that many students were facing similar challenges, particularly related to sleep patterns, academic pressure, phone usage at night, and long daily travel times to school.
This curiosity eventually grew into a small independent research effort, which later led me to develop a digital solution — the Student Sleep Health Analyzer.

Why I Built This
After collecting responses from students in my locality, I realized that simply identifying the problem was not enough. Many students were aware they were tired or stressed, but they did not have simple ways to reflect on their habits or understand how lifestyle factors were affecting their well-being.
Since it was not possible to personally guide every student, I decided to combine the programming skills I learned through CBSE Class 12 Python to create a simple digital tool that could help students evaluate their own sleep health.
My goal was to create something accessible, simple, and educational — a tool that encourages students to think about sleep habits and stress in a structured way.

Multilingual interface (English and Malayalam)

Academic focus prediction
Study planning suggestions based on sleep levels
Habit tracker to encourage healthy routines
Integrated feedback form for student responses

Methodology
The tool uses self-reported student inputs such as:
Hours of sleep per night
Night-time phone usage
Daily travel time to school

Using a simple sleep-debt model, the program estimates a stress score and provides categorized feedback such as:
Healthy
Moderate
At Risk
High Risk

The application is designed as a digital reflection tool, encouraging students to evaluate habits that may influence stress and academic focus.

Initial Research
Before building the tool, I conducted a small survey involving 30+ students in my locality to explore sleep patterns and perceived academic stress.
The findings helped guide the design of the application and identify which lifestyle factors students most commonly reported.

This project therefore combines:
student-led research
digital problem solving
community-focused impact

Impact Goal

Growing up with exposure to technological infrastructure in places like the UAE helped me see the potential of digital tools to improve everyday life.
Through this project, I wanted to apply that idea within my own community and contribute in a small way to digital health awareness among students.
The goal of the tool is not clinical diagnosis, but to encourage students to reflect on habits, understand stress patterns, and gradually build healthier routines.
